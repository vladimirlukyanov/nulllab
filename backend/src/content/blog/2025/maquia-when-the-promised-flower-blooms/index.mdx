## Introduction

Some animated films hit you like a gentle breeze.

Others punch you straight in the heart.

**Maquia: When the Promised Flower Blooms**, directed by **Mari Okada** and produced by **P.A. Works**, falls somewhere in between — a beautifully crafted story that lingers long after the credits roll.

<Picture
  width={1280}
  formats={['webp']}
  breakpoints={[370, 640, 960, 1280]}
  src={image2} alt="Maquia: When the Promised Flower Blooms – Introduction" />

This 2018 fantasy drama tells the story of **Maquia**, a young girl from a mysterious, long-lived race known as the Iorph. When her world is torn apart by war, she discovers a human infant, **Erial**, and raises him as her own.

At its heart, the film explores themes of **love, family, and the passage of time** — but with the emotional intensity of a tearjerker wrapped in a gorgeous fantasy world.

## Story and plot

The plot of **Maquia** centers on Maquia’s journey through a world where humans age quickly and wars rage endlessly. As an immortal being, Maquia faces the inevitable heartbreak of watching those she loves grow old and pass away while she remains the same.

The story balances intimate character moments with larger themes of war, loss, and the search for belonging. Maquia’s relationship with Erial drives the emotional core of the film, while the backdrop of a sprawling conflict adds stakes and tension.

<Picture
  width={1280}
  formats={['webp']}

The pacing is deliberate, allowing viewers to feel the weight of time passing. There are moments of quiet reflection, interspersed with bursts of action or magical spectacle, keeping the narrative engaging without ever feeling rushed.

While some may find the story melancholic, its depth and sincerity make it compelling rather than overwhelming.

Her calm demeanor masks immense strength, patience, and love — qualities that make her a truly unforgettable character.

The film does an excellent job portraying the challenges of parenthood and the inevitability of change.

### Supporting cast

Other characters, including the Iorph community and human warriors, enrich the story by illustrating the contrast between Maquia’s long-lived perspective and the fleeting lives of humans. Their motivations and struggles add nuance to the film’s exploration of time, mortality, and empathy.

## Animation and art style

Visually, **Maquia** is stunning.

The animation from **P.A. Works** combines fluid motion with richly detailed backgrounds. Fantasy landscapes, lush forests, and vibrant cities create a world that feels alive and immersive.

Character designs are expressive and help convey subtle emotions, while the magical elements — from glowing flowers to shimmering spells — enhance the story’s ethereal tone.

The film’s color palette often shifts to reflect mood: warm hues for quiet, tender moments and cooler tones for conflict or tragedy. This subtle visual storytelling amplifies the emotional impact.

## Sound and music

The music, composed by **Kenji Kawai**, perfectly complements the film’s emotional narrative. The score blends soft, melancholic melodies with sweeping orchestral passages, underscoring both intimate and epic moments.

Voice acting is equally strong. Maquia’s gentle and sometimes sorrowful voice captures her inner struggles, while Erial’s growth and changing perspective are conveyed naturally over time.

Sound design, from the whispers of the wind in forests to the clashing of swords in battle, immerses viewers fully into this fantasy world.

## Themes and messages

**Maquia: When the Promised Flower Blooms** explores several universal themes:

- **The passage of time**: Maquia’s immortality highlights the contrast between fleeting human life and the slow, enduring perspective of the Iorph.
- **Love and family**: The film examines the depth and pain of parental love, emphasizing that love often comes with inevitable loss.
- **War and humanity**: Conflict provides a backdrop that challenges Maquia and other characters, reinforcing the film’s exploration of empathy, survival, and morality.
- **Finding one’s place**: Maquia’s journey is as much about self-discovery as it is about caring for others, highlighting the importance of belonging and purpose.

## Personal opinion

Personally, I found **Maquia** to be a deeply moving film. It’s emotionally heavy at times, but the story is balanced with moments of hope, beauty, and tenderness.

The combination of stunning visuals, a heartfelt score, and compelling character development makes it one of the more memorable anime films of the last decade. Its emotional resonance is rare, and it has the power to make viewers reflect on their own relationships, the passage of time, and the fleeting nature of life.

While the film’s deliberate pacing may not suit everyone, those willing to immerse themselves in its bittersweet world will find it rewarding.

## Final verdict

**Maquia: When the Promised Flower Blooms** is a beautifully crafted, emotionally resonant story that blends fantasy with the universal truths of love, loss, and growth.

## Recommendation

I recommend **Maquia** for:

- fans of emotional, character-driven fantasy stories
- viewers who enjoy Studio Ghibli-esque visuals with a slightly darker, reflective tone
- anyone who appreciates narratives about family, love, and the passage of time

Prepare tissues — this film knows how to tug at your heartstrings — but in a way that leaves you thinking about it long after the story ends.
